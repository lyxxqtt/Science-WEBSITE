ECOLOGY

The scientific study of interactions between living organisms and their environment.

Introduction to Ecology

Ecology is a key branch of biology that examines how living organisms interact with one another and their surroundings. While each living organism is unique, they all share a crucial interdependence with each other and their environment. The term "ecology" was first coined by German biologist Ernst Haeckel, derived from the Greek words "oikos" (meaning "house" or "home") and "logos" (meaning "study"). Haeckel focused on understanding the relationship between organisms and the environment they inhabit.

Ecological Levels of Organization

Ecology not only studies the relationship between individual organisms and their environment but also explores the broader ecological systems, which are organized into various hierarchical levels. These levels are:

  • Organism: The basic unit of the ecological system, referring to an individual organism.
  • Population: A group of organisms of the same species living in the same area, capable of interbreeding and interacting.
  • Community: A group of different populations interacting in the same area.
  • Ecosystem: Comprises communities interacting with their physical environment. For example, a marine ecosystem includes fish, algae, and other marine life living together in the ocean.
  • Biome: A large geographical area characterized by specific types of plants and animals adapted to that environment.
  • Biosphere: The largest level of organization, encompassing all ecosystems on Earth and the interactions between them.

Components of the Ecosystem

The environment refers to all the living and nonliving factors that surround and influence an organism. Ecosystems consist of two major components: living (biotic) and nonliving (abiotic) factors.

Abiotic components include physical elements such as temperature, light, pressure, humidity, wind, precipitation, soil minerals, and other environmental factors.

Biotic components refer to the living organisms within the ecosystem, including plants, animals, fungi, algae, and microorganisms.
