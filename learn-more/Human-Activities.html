How do human activities affect the balance of the ecosystem?

An ecosystem constantly changes. Populations of organisms increase and decrease, trees fall, and animals die. The weather and climate also change over time. As these changes occur constantly, the adjustment in an ecosystem is required to maintain its balance. Sometimes the ecosystem is thrown out of balance abruptly by some natural calamities such as earthquakes, typhoons, forest fire, hurricanes, or volcanic eruption. In some cases, however, these rather disastrous events may help maintain the balance. For example, bush fires (or forest fires) may clear away old trees and shrubs and dead leaves and wood, creating a space for new plants to grow.

Human activities also affect the balance of the ecosystem. The damaging effects of these activities, such as excessive cutting of trees or dumping of poisonous chemicals on bodies of water, are quite evident. Some human activities that greatly affect the balance of the ecosystem are as follows:
